Welcome to fortune0

5 min read

You're reading the first fortune0 newsletter. If you're here, you probably believe the same thing we do: the best ideas shouldn't need permission to exist.

Traditional incubators pick winners. VCs pick winners. Accelerators pick winners. Someone, somewhere, decides which ideas deserve resources and attention. Everyone else gets a polite rejection email.

We think that's backwards.

What is fortune0?

fortune0 is a place where ideas live or die based on one thing: whether enough people believe in them to pledge support. No pitch decks. No warm intros. No gatekeepers.

Here's how it works:

  • Someone posts an idea. Could be a product, a project, a company, a movement—anything.
  • The clock starts. Every idea has a countdown. If it doesn't reach its pledge threshold before time runs out, it disappears.
  • People pledge. $1, $5, or $10. Not to buy anything—just to say "I think this should exist."
  • Ideas that hit their threshold survive. They get more time. More visibility. A chance to become real.

Think of it like natural selection for ideas, except the environment is human attention and belief.

Why this matters

Every day, thousands of good ideas die in someone's Notes app. The person who had the idea either didn't know the right people, didn't have the resources, or just didn't have the energy to navigate the gatekeeping.

Meanwhile, mediocre ideas with good connections get funded all the time.

fortune0 is an experiment in fixing that asymmetry. What if the only thing that mattered was whether strangers on the internet thought your idea was worth $1 of belief?

This week's ideas to watch

Check out the ideas page to see what's currently fighting for survival. Some will make it. Most won't. That's the point.

What's next

This is just the beginning. In the coming weeks, we'll be:

  • Opening up idea submissions to everyone
  • Building tools for idea creators to rally support
  • Experimenting with what happens when ideas survive—do they become companies? Communities? Something else?

If you have thoughts, reply to this email. Seriously. We read everything.

See you next week.

—Matt
